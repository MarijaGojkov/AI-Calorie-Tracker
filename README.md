# AI-Calorie-Tracker

Building AI course project

## Summary

AI-Calorie-Tracker is an AI-powered nutrition assistant that helps users estimate their daily calorie needs, track food intake, and receive personalized recommendations based on their age, sex, height, weight, activity level, and goals. The purpose is to make healthy eating and weight management easier and more accessible.

## Background

Many people struggle with understanding how many calories they should consume each day.

Common problems include:

* Estimating maintenance calories incorrectly
* Following unrealistic diets
* Not understanding how activity affects calorie needs
* Difficulty tracking long-term progress

My motivation comes from my interest in nutrition, health, and technology. Many calorie calculators provide generic recommendations, while AI can offer more personalized guidance based on individual characteristics and behavior.

## How is it used?

The user enters:

* Age
* Sex
* Height
* Weight
* Activity level
* Goal (lose weight, maintain weight, gain weight)

The system estimates calorie requirements and suggests a daily calorie target.

The user can also record meals and monitor progress over time.

Potential users include:

* People trying to lose weight
* People trying to gain weight
* Fitness enthusiasts
* Anyone interested in healthier eating habits

## Data sources and AI methods

Possible data sources:

* User-provided information
* Food nutrition databases
* Historical calorie tracking data

AI methods that could be used:

* Regression models for calorie estimation
* Classification models for identifying suitable calorie targets
* Recommendation systems for meal suggestions
* Natural Language Processing (NLP) for food descriptions entered as text

Example input:

```
Age: 25
Sex: Female
Height: 158 cm
Weight: 68 kg
Activity: Moderate
Goal: Weight loss
```

Example output:

```
Estimated maintenance calories: 2100 kcal
Recommended calorie target: 1600 kcal
```

## Challenges

This project cannot replace professional medical advice.

Limitations include:

* Inaccurate food logging
* Individual metabolic differences
* Medical conditions that affect calorie requirements
* Limited availability of high-quality nutrition data

Ethical considerations include protecting user privacy and avoiding unhealthy dieting recommendations.

## What next?

Future improvements could include:
